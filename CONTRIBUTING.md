# Contributing to Documentation-Driven Development

First of all — thank you. This manifesto exists to be challenged, improved, and evolved by the community.

---

## Ways to Contribute

### 📝 Improve the manifesto
- Fix typos, clarify wording, improve examples
- Propose new sections or concepts
- Translate the manifesto into other languages

### 💡 Share your experience
- Open an issue describing how DDD worked (or didn't) for your team
- Add a real-world case study to the manifesto
- Share edge cases, anti-patterns, or lessons learned

- Improve accessibility or performance
- Suggest new sections or visual improvements

### ⚖️ Contribute to the Living Knowledge License discussion
- Open an issue with your thoughts on licensing AI-co-authored documentation
- Reference existing legal frameworks that could apply
- Propose a draft license structure

---

## How to Contribute

1. **Fork** this repository
2. **Create a branch** — `git checkout -b my-contribution`
3. **Make your changes** — edit `README.md` (the manifesto) or `index.html` (the site)
4. **Commit with a clear message** — describe what you changed and why
5. **Open a Pull Request** — describe your contribution and link any relevant issues

---

## Contribution Philosophy

This repo is itself a DDD project. That means:

- Every significant change should be **discussed in an issue first**
- Pull requests should explain the **why**, not just the what
- The manifesto evolves through **community consensus**, not unilateral edits
- All contributors are credited — human and AI alike

---

## Code of Conduct

Be kind. Be clear. Be constructive.
This is a manifesto about making software development better — that starts here.
